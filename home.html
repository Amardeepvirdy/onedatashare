Beta, you say?

Yes, StorkCloud is developed by a small group of graduate students at the University at Buffalo who lack the human resources to establish and maintain a dedicated quality assurance team. Instead, we're trying something a little unusual — starting a public beta testing phase without thorough internal testing.

This means StorkCloud is not only experimental, it is very experimental. The system is liable to fail, crash, stall, leave transfers partially complete, forget about jobs, be taken down for intermittent periods while we fix problems, not support certain remote servers, or otherwise not work properly.

The user inferface is also very unpolished and will likely do some strange or surprising things. Many actions do not currently give any visual indicators that they are in progess or have completed, and some interface elements are nonfunctional placeholders or are buggy.

For the next few months until StorkCloud has reached an acceptable level of stability, it should not be used for any mission-critical operations or be trusted with any sensitive information. We will, however, greatly appreciate any bug reports or other feedback from testers. Bug reports can be submitted here.

Furthermore, as an academic endeavour, StorkCloud will be used as a testing platform for various experimental scheduling and optimization algorithms and transfer protocols. Such features should be used with caution. New features introduced in the future will be marked in a conspicuous way to indicate their experimental status.

That said, StorkCloud will be undergoing many changes during this testing period. New features will be introduced and problems will be fixed as we get to them.

The scheduler component underlying the system is open source. We invite developers to contribute patches and new features through pull requests to our our GitHub repository: https://github.com/didclab/stork

User Accounts

Until StorkCloud is announced stable, we reserve the right to reset or rollback the server state at any time for testing or debugging purposes. As a result, user accounts may vanish without warning, and with them all transfer history and stored credentials.

We will also be removing user accounts at the end of beta testing, so anything that happens during beta testing will not be carried over to the final release.
